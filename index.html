Maasai Mara

The Maasai Mara is the beating heart of East Africa's wildlife heritage, an open savanna where the Great Migration carries over a million wildebeest and zebra across the Mara River each year. It shares an unbroken border with Tanzania's Serengeti and takes its name from the Maasai people, whose cattle-herding culture, beadwork and age-old relationship with this land continue alongside the lion, elephant and cheetah that draw travellers from across the world. For those interested in wildlife, conservation and living Maasai tradition.

Extension: 1,510 km²
Location: Great Rift Valley, southwestern Kenya Link: Google maps ↗

Who we design for

Inclusion is a product surface — not a charity footnote. These four pillars shape parks, stays, artifacts, guides, and checkout.

Women & children

Family-safe stay tags, women-led artisan shops (e.g. Kazuri Beads), and guide pairing that prioritizes trusted student hosts for mothers traveling with children.

Youth

University student guides from 12 Kenyan campuses earn directly — turning local knowledge into paid work in a country where FKE cites 67% of ages 15–34 without adequate employment.

People with disabilities

Accessible park & lodge filters, access-trained student guides, tactile craft descriptions, captions on heritage video, and a calm reduced-motion mode for maps and animations.

Language belonging

Voice + text with ElevenLabs across 74 global languages and 6 African languages — plus Swahili/English checkout copy for M-Pesa so payment never depends on card English alone.

What we’re building into the product

Concrete Aurora Access commitments across every page.

  • Digital access — keyboard & screen-reader paths for Parks, Artifacts, Guides, and M-Pesa STK status; high-contrast and larger text; reduced motion.
  • Accessible travel data — wheelchair / vehicle-safari / step-free lodge fields and an “Accessible stays & parks” filter on Travel & Stay.
  • Accessible crafts — audio & text descriptions of materials and making; shops tagged for seated or hotel-delivery shopping.
  • Access companions — student guides badged for mobility, low-vision, or sign-language support; pairing preference in Guides.
  • Women-led & family journeys — highlight women-run cooperatives and child-friendly itineraries without separating them from the main marketplace.
  • Youth livelihoods — guide fees stay with Kenyan students; artisan network growth creates county-level craft work.
