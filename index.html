Maasai Mara

The Maasai Mara is the beating heart of East Africa's wildlife heritage, an open savanna where the Great Migration carries over a million wildebeest and zebra across the Mara River each year. It shares an unbroken border with Tanzania's Serengeti and takes its name from the Maasai people, whose cattle-herding culture, beadwork and age-old relationship with this land continue alongside the lion, elephant and cheetah that draw travellers from across the world. For those interested in wildlife, conservation and living Maasai tradition.
